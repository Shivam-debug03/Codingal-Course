Mini Biography - Michael Jackson

Michael Joseph Jackson (Born August 29, 1958 (Gary, Indiana) and Died June 25, 2009 (Los Angeles, California)).

Michael Joseph Jackson was an American singer, songwriter, and dancer. Dubbed the "King of Pop", he is regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century.

Jackson's contributions to music, dance, and fashion, along with his publicized personal life, made him a global figure in popular culture for over four decades.

The Early Years & The Jackson 5

Beginnings: He started performing as a child prodigy and became the lead singer of his family's group, the Jackson 5, with Motown hits like "I Want You Back".

Breakthrough: The Jackson 5's success paved the way for his solo career, which began in the early 1970s.

Solo Career & Major Achievements


The Thriller Phenomenon: After going solo, his 1982 album Thriller became—and remains—the best-selling album of all time.

Visual Storytelling: He revolutionized the medium of music videos with cinematic and complex productions for songs like

"Billie Jean," "Beat It," and "Thriller"

Dance, Style & Legacy

Signature Moves: He popularized intricate dance routines, most notably his famous glide known as the

"Moonwalk".

Trademarks: Known for his signature aesthetic, including a single sequined white glove, black fedora, and white socks with black shoes.

Humanitarianism/Activism

Beyond music, he actively supported humanitarian causes and wrote benefit songs like "We Are the World" (with Lionel Richie) to raise millions for famine relief.

"If you wanna make the world a better place, take a look at yourself, and make the change."
